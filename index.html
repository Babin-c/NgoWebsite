Our Story

Founded in 2010, Silent Voices began as a small community initiative by a group of deaf activists and hearing allies who recognized the systemic barriers faced by the deaf community in our society.

What started as sign language classes in a community center has grown into a nationwide movement with programs in education, employment, accessibility advocacy, and cultural preservation.

Today, we serve over 50,000 individuals annually through our various programs and initiatives, making us one of the leading organizations for deaf empowerment in the country.

Our Core Values

  • Inclusion

    We believe in a society where deaf individuals fully participate in all aspects of life without barriers.

  • Equality

    We advocate for equal rights and opportunities for deaf people in education, employment, and social participation.

  • Cultural Pride

    We celebrate deaf culture and sign language as vital parts of human diversity.

  • Empowerment

    We equip deaf individuals with the skills, knowledge, and confidence to advocate for themselves.

  • Collaboration

    We work in partnership with the deaf community, hearing allies, and other organizations to create systemic change.

  • Innovation

    We develop creative solutions to address the unique challenges faced by the deaf community.
